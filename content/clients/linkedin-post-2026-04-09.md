## Post Content

Full project context on day one. That's the whole pitch for Clarity, the agentic intelligence framework I just open-sourced.

Here's the problem I kept hitting: every new engineering engagement starts from zero. You spend weeks just understanding the client's stack before you can actually be useful. And by the time you're ramped up, you've burned through budget that should've gone to building.

So I built something about it.

The seed was Andrej Karpathy's LLM Wiki pattern. Structured knowledge that an AI reads before answering. Smart idea. But a wiki alone is static, and projects aren't.

Clarity layers three things on top. Operational data in a structured YAML file that captures project state right now, not last quarter. Behavioral memory that tracks how a team actually prefers to work (because every team is different and nobody writes that down). And a self-learn loop where raw observations validate against live state and auto-promote into real knowledge. The system gets smarter every session without you manually curating it.

9 slash commands. Obsidian-compatible wiki. Works with Claude Code out of the box.

Why give it away? Because the hard part of agentic AI isn't the model. It's the context. And most teams are still solving that with copy-pasted Slack threads and tribal knowledge that lives in one person's head.

That's a terrible system. This is a better one.
